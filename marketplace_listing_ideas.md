## Repairs

### String Trimmers / Weedeaters

**Weedeater Won't Start? — Lorena/Waco Repair**
Pulling and pulling with nothing happening usually means carb or fuel line trouble, not a dead trimmer — we can usually get it running same week.

**String Trimmer Repair — Cheaper Than a New One**
Before you drop money on a new weedeater, let us take a look — most trimmer issues are a quick, inexpensive fix.

**Trimmer Won't Feed Line — Fast Fix**
If the head just spins and spins without feeding string, it's almost always a worn spring or tangled spool — we fix it while you wait.

**Weedeater Bogs Down and Dies in Tall Grass**
If your trimmer runs fine at idle but dies the second it hits thick weeds, that's a carburetor or air filter problem we see constantly.

**String Trimmer Smoking — Don't Ignore It**
Blue or white smoke usually points to a fuel mix or oil seal issue — catch it early before it turns into a bigger repair.

**Trimmer Engine Revs but Cutting Head Won't Spin**
If the motor sounds fine but the head just sits there when you squeeze the throttle, that's usually a worn clutch or drive shaft, not a dead trimmer.

**Weedeater Fouling Spark Plugs Constantly**
If you're replacing the plug every few weeks and it's still running rough, that's usually a carburetor tuned too rich, not a bad plug.

**Trimmer String Keeps Breaking or Snapping**
Constant line breakage is often a bushing or line-guide issue, not the string itself — we can diagnose it quickly.

**Weedeater Won't Idle — Stalls at Low RPM**
If it runs fine at full throttle but dies the moment you let off, that's a classic idle-mixture or air leak symptom.

**Weedeater Hard to Start Cold or Hot**
Whether it's a cold-morning fight or won't restart when warm, both point to specific carb issues we deal with all the time.

**Trimmer Spring Tune-Up — Get It Running Before Mowing Season**
Sat in the shed all winter and now won't start or runs rough? Old fuel gums up the carb — we'll get it back in shape before the grass takes over.

**String Trimmer Shaking or Vibrating Badly**
Excess vibration usually means a loose or worn drive shaft coupling — worth fixing before it damages the head or lets the shaft snap.

**Weedeater Leaking Gas — Safety and Repair**
A leaking fuel line or tank seal isn't just annoying, it's a fire risk — we'll track down the leak and seal it up.

**Trimmer Backfires or Pops When Starting**
Backfiring on startup is often timing or a bad spark arrestor — an easy fix that keeps it from getting worse.

**Weedeater Throttle Stuck or Won't Return to Idle**
A throttle that sticks wide open or won't spring back is a safety issue as much as a repair one — we'll free it up and get the return spring working right.

**Trimmer Loses Power Halfway Through the Yard**
If it starts strong and fades as you go, that's usually a clogged fuel filter or dirty carb starving it of fuel.

### Push Mowers

**Push Mower Won't Start — Lorena/Waco Repair**
No spark, no fire, or just cranks and cranks — nine times out of ten it's the carburetor or a fouled spark plug, both quick fixes.

**Push Mower Runs Then Dies — Fast Diagnosis**
Starts fine, then quits within a minute or two? That's a classic fuel-starvation symptom we can usually fix same visit.

**Push Mower Self-Propel Not Working**
If the wheels won't pull anymore, it's typically a worn drive cable or belt, not a reason to buy a whole new mower.

**Mower Blade Won't Engage**
No blade engagement usually traces back to a bad cable, worn belt, or the blade brake clutch — all repairable.

**Push Mower Repair — Cheaper Than Buying New**
Before you haul it to the curb, let us look at it. Most push mowers we see need one part, not a replacement.

**Push Mower Cutting Uneven — Stripes or Missed Patches**
Scalping in some spots and missing grass in others usually means an uneven deck or a bent blade, both easy corrections.

**Push Mower Won't Stay Running at Idle**
Dies as soon as you let go of the throttle? That points to a dirty carb or bad idle adjustment.

**Push Mower Smoking Under the Hood**
White smoke on startup after sitting, or steady blue smoke while running — both are diagnosable and usually a quick repair.

**Push Mower Pull Cord Broken or Won't Retract**
A snapped cord or one that won't snap back is a simple recoil-spring fix, not a reason to replace the whole mower.

**Mower Shaking or Vibrating Badly While Running**
Bad vibration is often a bent or unbalanced blade — left alone it can wreck the engine mount.

**Push Mower Won't Start After Sitting All Winter**
Old gas gums up the carburetor over winter storage — a cleaning and fresh fuel usually has it running again before spring.

**Push Mower Leaking Oil or Gas**
A leak under the deck or by the tank is usually a gasket or seal issue — we'll find it and stop it before it gets worse.

**Push Mower Backfires When You Shut It Off**
Backfiring on shutdown is a common and fixable ignition timing issue.

**Push Mower Bogs Down in Thick or Wet Grass**
If it chokes out in taller grass but runs fine on short stuff, that's a power or airflow issue, often the air filter or carb.

**Push Mower Losing Power the Longer You Mow**
Starts strong but fades after ten or fifteen minutes of mowing? That's usually a clogged fuel filter or a carburetor that can't keep up, not a mower that's simply worn out.

**Push Mower Won't Hold Choke or Floods on Startup**
Flooding on every start attempt is almost always a stuck choke or carb float issue — a straightforward fix.

### Riding Mowers

**Riding Mower Won't Start — Lorena/Waco Repair**
Clicking, cranking but no fire, or dead silence — could be the battery, solenoid, or safety switch. We'll find it fast.

**Riding Mower Deck Won't Engage**
If the blades won't kick on when you flip the PTO switch, it's usually the clutch, a blown fuse, or a bad switch — all fixable.

**Riding Mower Repair — Before You Buy New**
A new rider is a big purchase. Most of the ones we see are one belt or switch away from running fine again.

**Riding Mower Belt Keeps Slipping or Coming Off**
A deck belt that won't stay put is usually a worn idler pulley or bad belt tension — we'll get it tracking right.

**Riding Mower Won't Move — Transmission or Hydro Issue**
Engine runs but the mower won't drive forward or reverse — that's a drive belt or hydrostatic transmission problem, not a lost cause.

**Riding Mower Cutting Uneven or Leaving Stripes**
Scalped in spots, missed in others — deck leveling and blade balance are the usual culprits, and it's a quick adjustment.

**Riding Mower Won't Start — Seat Safety Switch**
If it cranks but won't fire unless you're standing up wiggling the seat, the safety interlock switch is likely bad.

**Riding Mower Smoking From Under the Hood**
Blue smoke on startup or steady white smoke while mowing — both point to specific, diagnosable engine issues.

**Riding Mower Loses Power Going Uphill**
Bogging down on slopes but fine on flat ground usually means a fuel delivery or governor issue.

**Riding Mower Blows a Fuse Every Time You Start It**
Repeated blown fuses point to a short somewhere in the wiring harness — worth tracking down before it strands you mid-mow.

**Riding Mower Battery Won't Hold a Charge**
If it cranks slow or needs a jump every time you mow, a weak battery or bad charging system is probably why — cheap to diagnose, cheaper than a new mower.

**Riding Mower Backfires on Shutdown**
A pop or bang when you turn the key off is a common timing issue, easy to correct.

**Riding Mower Vibrating Badly at the Deck**
Excess shake at the deck is often a bent blade or worn spindle bearing — left unchecked it can crack the deck housing.

**Riding Mower Spring Tune-Up Before Mowing Season**
Sat in the barn all winter and now cranks but won't fire? Stale fuel and a gummed carb are the usual cause — get ahead of the season.

**Riding Mower Steering Feels Loose or Won't Turn Right**
Sloppy or one-sided steering is usually worn tie rods or a steering gear issue — both straightforward to repair.

**Riding Mower Dies the Moment the Blades Engage**
If the engine stalls out only when you drop the deck, that's a classic sign of an underpowered or misadjusted carburetor.

### Zero-Turn Mowers

**Zero-Turn Won't Turn One Direction**
If it spins fine one way but drags or won't pivot the other, that's almost always a hydro-drive or steering lever adjustment issue.

**Zero-Turn Deck Won't Engage — PTO Clutch Issue**
Blades not kicking on when you engage the PTO usually means a worn electric clutch, not a mower on its way out.

**Zero-Turn Mower Repair — Before You Buy a New One**
Zero-turns aren't cheap. Most hydro-drive and deck issues we see are a repair, not a replacement.

**Zero-Turn Hydrostatic Drive Pulling to One Side**
Drifting left or right when you push both levers evenly points to a hydro-drive linkage that's out of adjustment.

**Zero-Turn Won't Hold RPM Under Load**
Engine bogs down the moment the deck engages or you hit thicker grass — usually a carb or governor problem.

**Zero-Turn Deck Belt Slipping or Squealing**
A belt that slips, squeals, or throws off track is usually tension or a worn pulley — a quick fix that saves the belt.

**Zero-Turn Cutting Uneven Side to Side**
One side cutting higher than the other means the deck is out of level — a routine adjustment most owners can't do themselves.

**Zero-Turn Won't Start — Seat or Safety Switch**
Cranks but won't fire unless you're perfectly seated? That's the safety interlock circuit, not the engine.

**Zero-Turn Smoking Under Load**
Smoke that shows up only when the deck's engaged and under load points to a specific, fixable overload issue.

**Zero-Turn Steering Levers Drifting Off Center**
If the control levers won't return to neutral cleanly, that's a linkage or spring adjustment we can dial back in.

**Zero-Turn Losing Power on Hills or Inclines**
Bogging down on slopes points to fuel delivery or a governor issue that flat-ground testing won't reveal.

**Zero-Turn Blade Spindle Making Noise**
Grinding or squealing from the spindle is a bearing on its way out — catch it before it costs you a blade.

**Zero-Turn Backfires or Pops on Shutdown**
A backfire when you kill the engine is a timing issue, usually a fast, inexpensive correction.

**Zero-Turn Leaking Hydraulic Fluid**
A puddle under the drive wheels usually means a hydro line or seal has failed — worth fixing before the drive fails completely.

**Zero-Turn Transmission Running Hot**
If the hydro-drive feels sluggish after mowing a while, overheating fluid is often the cause — a fixable maintenance issue.

**Zero-Turn Spring Check-Up Before Cutting Season**
Sat all winter and now hesitates to engage the deck or hold RPM? Get it looked at before the grass outgrows your schedule.

### Tillers

**Tiller Tines Won't Turn — Lorena/Waco Repair**
Engine runs fine but the tines just sit there — that's almost always a slipping belt or worn drive clutch, an easy fix.

**Tiller Transmission Won't Engage**
If shifting into gear does nothing, the gearbox or shift linkage needs attention — not a reason to replace the whole tiller.

**Tiller Repair — Before You Buy New**
Most tillers we see need a belt, clutch, or carb cleaning — far cheaper than a brand-new machine.

**Tiller Stalls the Moment Tines Hit the Ground**
Runs fine at idle but dies under load in the dirt — that's a classic fuel delivery or carburetor issue.

**Tiller Won't Start — Sat All Winter**
Old fuel gums up the carburetor fast. If it won't fire after sitting in the shed, that's usually the culprit, not the engine itself.

**Tiller Tines Turn But Don't Dig**
If the tines spin but barely bite into the soil, a slipping belt or worn clutch is robbing your power.

**Tiller Vibrating or Shaking Badly**
Excess shake usually means a bent tine shaft or worn bearing — left alone it can crack the housing.

**Tiller Pulls Hard to One Side**
If it fights you constantly in one direction, uneven tine wear or a bent shaft is usually to blame.

**Tiller Smoking While Running**
Smoke from a tiller under load often points to an overworked engine or oil issue — worth catching early.

**Tiller Leaking Gas or Oil**
A leak near the tank or crankcase is a gasket or seal problem, and it's a straightforward repair.

**Tiller Backfires on Startup**
Popping when you pull-start it is a timing issue that's quick to correct.

**Tiller Tines Jammed With Grass or Vines**
Wrapped-up grass, vines, or roots around the tine shaft will stall a tiller dead — a quick clear-out, and sometimes a straightened tine, is usually all it takes.

**Tiller Won't Idle Without Stalling**
Dies at idle every time — classic carb adjustment symptom, especially after sitting unused.

**Tiller Forward/Reverse Won't Shift**
A tiller stuck in one gear or that grinds when shifting needs linkage or gearbox attention before it strands you mid-row.

**Tiller Chain or Belt Drive Slipping**
Power loss to the tines with a chain-drive or belt-drive tiller is usually a tension or wear issue, cheap to fix.

**Tiller Handlebars Loose or Hard to Control**
If it fights you or wanders side to side while you're tilling, loose handlebar bolts or a worn mounting bracket are usually to blame — an easy adjustment.

### Generators

**Generator Won't Hold a Load — Lorena/Waco Repair**
Runs fine with nothing plugged in but drops or dies the moment you add a real load — that's a governor or carb issue we see all the time.

**Generator Won't Start After Sitting**
Stale gas gums up the carburetor fast in a generator that only runs occasionally — a cleaning usually solves it.

**Generator Repair — Before You Buy New**
A new generator is a real investment. Most of the ones we see just need a carb cleaning or fuel system flush.

**Generator Transfer Switch Not Working Right**
If power isn't switching over cleanly during an outage, the transfer switch itself may need service, not the generator.

**Generator Leaking Oil or Gas**
A puddle under the unit or a fuel smell while it's running points to a gasket or fuel line issue — worth fixing fast, especially before you need it in a storm.

**Generator Surging or RPM Fluctuating**
An engine that revs up and down on its own usually has a carb or governor problem, easy to diagnose.

**Generator Won't Produce Power at All**
Engine runs great but nothing comes out of the outlets — that points to the windings or voltage regulator, not necessarily a dead unit.

**Generator Trips Its Breaker and Won't Reset**
Repeated tripping usually means an overload condition or a failing breaker itself — worth checking before you rely on it in a storm.

**Generator Shuts Off After a Few Minutes**
Starts fine, then dies shortly after — often a low-oil sensor issue or a fuel starvation problem, both fixable.

**Generator Fall Check-Up Before Storm Season**
Before the next outage hits, make sure yours actually starts and holds a load — most failures happen exactly when you need it most.

**Generator Hard to Start When Cold**
A generator that fights you every cold morning usually needs a choke or carb adjustment.

**Generator Backfires or Pops When Running**
Backfiring under load points to timing or a carb mixture issue that's worth correcting before it damages the engine.

**Generator Smoking on Startup**
White smoke at startup after storage versus steady smoke while running point to different issues — we can tell you which you've got.

**Generator Low-Oil Light Won't Clear**
If the low-oil shutdown keeps tripping even after you've topped it off, the sensor itself may be the problem.

**Generator Voltage Fluctuating Under Load**
Lights dimming or flickering when appliances kick on points to a voltage regulator issue, not a generator that's simply too small.

**Generator Pull Cord Broken or Won't Retract**
A snapped cord or one that won't snap back is a quick recoil-spring fix, not a reason to replace a generator that's otherwise fine.
